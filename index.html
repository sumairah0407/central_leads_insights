08
What's working and what isn't
Key findings from both the leads pipeline and conversions data
Working well
App is the only conversion engine
88 of 125 clients (70%) from Mobile App. ₹39.7 Cr AUM (63% of total). 1.41% conv rate vs 0.06% for Meta. When app campaign paused, conversions dropped to zero — proven by the App_1 sheet data. This channel must be protected at all budget levels.
30–90 day TAT is the sweet spot
Leads converting in 30–90 days generated ₹31.9 Cr (51% of all AUM) at an avg ₹72L per client. The 90–120d band averages ₹104.5L — your best ticket size. Leads genuinely need 1–3 months of RM nurturing before committing.
Wallet deepening compounds AUM
54% of converted clients added assets after their first transaction. Deepened clients hold ₹73.8L avg vs ₹23.5L for single-transaction clients — a 3.1× multiplier. The programme's long-term AUM growth is driven by this cohort, not new names.
RM books convert 59× better
109 of 125 conversions from RM-owned pipelines at 5.2% conv rate. Acquisition Team: 0.09%. The gap isn't lead quality or product — it's relationship ownership. The same demographic converts dramatically better when an RM is accountable for the relationship.
Google SIF — highest intent paid channel
1.94% conversion rate, 11.1% relevant rate. People searching "SIF" or "Ionic Wealth" are already in late-stage decision mode. High intent baked into the search query itself. Significantly underfunded relative to Meta.
PMS and AOL Stocks produce bigger tickets
PMS clients: avg ₹75.1L (vs overall ₹50.5L). AOL Stocks: avg ₹80.8L. Clients who enter via higher-complexity products are disproportionately more valuable. The product mix at conversion matters as much as the channel.
Not working
Meta Paid: 1,613 leads, 6 conversions
0.06% conversion rate. Allocate vserv: 41% bad lead rate, 2.7% relevant rate. Allocate remarketing: 34% bad leads. The audience definition is broken — reaching people who misunderstand the product, are below minimum ticket size, or submitted the form by accident.
Budget cuts cause double damage
Your observation is statistically confirmed. Low-volume weeks (<200 leads/week) see relevant rates fall to 3.9% from a peak of 12%. Meta's algorithm exits the learned audience when impressions drop. Volume and quality degrade simultaneously — there is a minimum viable budget floor.
69% of leads never properly reached
App leads connect at 11.6% but 51% are relevant when reached. Website: 4.3% connect, 54% relevant-of-connected. These are the highest-intent people in the pipeline and they're not being called. No calling SLA exists for high-pulled-portfolio leads.

Top 3 priority actions
Highest-leverage interventions based on the combined data
01
Escalate 21 Hot Leads with pulled portfolios today
₹14.6 Cr in pulled AUM, category = Hot Lead, not yet converted. These are the highest-intent leads in the system. They should not be in a telecaller queue — assign directly to the relevant RM with a 24-hour contact SLA. This is the fastest path to near-term AUM.
02
Protect App campaign budget at all scenarios
88/125 conversions, ₹39.7 Cr AUM, 1.41% conv rate. The paused-weeks data proves zero conversions when the campaign is off. If budget must be cut anywhere, cut Meta Allocate first (1 conversion from 650 leads). App campaign ROI is non-negotiable to protect.
03
Create a calling SLA for App + Website leads with pulled AUM >₹10L
51–54% relevant-of-connected rate on these two channels — the problem is reach, not quality. Mandate ≥3 call attempts in the first 48 hours for any lead with a pulled portfolio above ₹10L. 190 such leads are currently unreached. Fixing this costs nothing.
