What's working and what isn't
Management assessment — grounded strictly in the numbers, not expectation
working
App is the programme's only true conversion engine
87 of 125 clients (70%) from organic Mobile App. ₹39.3 Cr AUM (62%). 8.74% conversion rate. When the app install campaign was paused (3 consecutive weeks in the data), conversions went to zero. Restoring it restored conversions. The causal relationship is clear and non-controversial.
30–120 day TAT produces the real AUM
₹42.4 Cr (67% of total) came from leads converting at 30–120 days. The 90–120 day band averages ₹104.5 L per client — highest of any band. Rushed conversions (0–15d) average ₹15 L. This programme rewards sustained RM relationship-building; it is not a quick-close operation.
Wallet deepening is a 3× AUM multiplier
54% of converted clients added assets after their first transaction. Deepened clients: ₹73.8 L avg. Single-transaction: ₹23.5 L. The long-term value of the programme compounds through this cohort. It needs to be actively managed — the RM relationship that produces first investment must be maintained post-onboarding.
AOL produces the best consistent ticket outside of App
8 conversions at ₹92.8 L avg, median ₹86.2 L — the number is not skewed by one client. The Streamliner email campaigns are finding genuine HNIs with established corpus and a willingness to evaluate a new service. Low frequency but high per-client value — the economics per rupee spent deserve scrutiny vs Meta.
RM ownership produces 33× better outcomes
When a named RM owns the lead relationship, 63% become relevant. When the Acquisition Team handles it, 1.9% do. The product is not the constraint — the engagement structure is. This is the most important structural insight in the report and the one with the most actionable consequence.
MyGate is the highest-quality untapped channel
35.8% relevant rate, 73% relevant-of-connected. Zero conversions only because the channel is too new and has too few leads for one to materialise yet. The audience profile is correct — gated community residents match Ionic's ICP. Scale here before allocating more to Meta.
not working
Meta at scale is generating junk — its AUM number is misleading
5,339 leads, 7 conversions, 0.13% conversion rate. ₹8.1 Cr of the ₹10 Cr AUM is a single client via PA Remarketing (Sushant Dash). Excluding him: 6 clients, ₹1.9 Cr. Allocate vserv has a 41% bad lead rate and near-zero relevance. The audience definition is fundamentally wrong — the leads being generated do not have the profile, intent, or corpus for Ionic's products at scale.
Budget cuts cause non-linear damage to quality
The data confirms your hypothesis: volume and quality decline together when budgets drop. This is Meta's algorithm losing its learned audience. Weeks below 200 leads see 5.1% relevant rate vs 8.4% above 300 leads. There is a minimum viable budget floor — cutting below it doesn't linearly reduce cost-per-lead; it structurally degrades cost-per-relevant-lead. Budget decisions should be modelled on cost-per-relevant-lead, not cost-per-lead.
The calling operation is leaving the best pipeline unworked
App leads: 11.6% connect rate, 51% relevant when reached. Website: 4.3% connect, 54% relevant. ₹198 Cr sits in Warm Lead + Couldn't connect categories with pulled portfolios above ₹50L. Pooja Kapoor (₹51.6 Cr) has never been called — she is a New Lead. There is no calling SLA. This is the largest recoverable inefficiency in the programme and requires no incremental marketing spend to address.

Platform stories — what worked and what didn't
Platform and campaign level success/failure analysis with specific evidence
success stories
App campaign: the one non-negotiable
87 of 125 conversions (70%). ₹39.3 Cr AUM. 8.74% conversion rate — nearly 4× any other channel. When paused (3 consecutive weeks in Q1 2026), conversions went to zero. Restarting restored them. The causal mechanism is clear: the app install campaign drives high-intent users to download, pull their portfolio, and then engage with the RM.

The channel is organic — the spend is on driving downloads, not on conversion itself. This makes its ROI effectively infinite against just conversion activity. Budget this as infrastructure, not discretionary spend.
Google SIF: the fastest-converting paid channel
155 leads, 24 relevant (15.5%), 3 conversions, avg ₹45.6 day TAT — 50% faster than Meta or App. ₹17.6L spend, ₹1.76 Cr AUM from SIF alone (10× ROI from this one campaign).

The mechanism: people searching "SIF fund", "strategic investment fund India" or "Ionic SIF" are already in an active research phase. They've already decided to invest in the category; they're just choosing a provider. This is the highest-intent paid audience Ionic has access to.

Google SIF can't scale infinitely (search volume is finite) but it can scale 2–3× more than current budget without quality dilution.
Accredited Investor campaign (paused): the unfinished experiment
363 leads, 62 relevant (17.1%), CPRL ₹11,791 — the most efficient CPRL of any Meta campaign. Was paused. The audience (SEBI accredited investors) is self-qualified by regulatory definition — they've certified minimum net worth. Reactivating this campaign at even ₹5L/month should produce significantly better relevant rates than the current Allocate campaigns.
AOL Streamliner email: highest ticket size per conversion
8 conversions, avg ₹92.8 L, median ₹86.2 L — both measures confirm quality is real. The mechanism: people who open an email about their portfolio assessment are already engaged with their own wealth management. They're not passive scrollers. This is interruption marketing at its most efficient. 8 clients generating ₹7.4 Cr from essentially zero marginal ad spend.
what isn't working
Meta Allocate campaigns: spending money to find the wrong audience
Allocate vserv: 258 leads, 7 relevant (2.7%), 41% bad lead rate, CPRL ₹38,091. Allocate Meta Form Fill: 682 leads, 52 relevant (7.6%), 34% bad lead rate.

The Allocate product has a high minimum ticket size and requires a specific investor profile. Meta's broad audience targeting — even with lookalikes — is finding people who don't meet basic eligibility: wrong ticket size, wrong investor profile, or outright junk entries. Every rupee on Meta Allocate produces a worse outcome than the same rupee on Google SIF or the App campaign.

The PMAX problem is embedded here too: 176 leads from PMAX, zero relevant, zero conversions. Should be off.
LinkedIn: ₹26L spent, 0 conversions
557 LinkedIn leads in the all-time dataset (Ads + organic), zero conversions recorded. The audience (job seniority targeting, decision-makers) has the right demographic profile on paper — but the conversion mechanics don't work. LinkedIn users engage professionally, not in a wealth management mindset. The form fill rate is there (742 leads over Mar–Sep 25) but the follow-through to investment hasn't happened.

₹26L spent with zero AUM return. Either needs a completely different creative approach (SEBI accreditation angle) or pause and redeploy to App/Google SIF.
Meta's budget sensitivity: the floor problem
The data is unambiguous: weeks with Meta spend below ₹1.5L produce relevant rates of 3.9–5.1%. Weeks above ₹2.5L produce 8–12%. This isn't random noise — it's Meta's algorithm losing its learned audience when daily budgets fall below the threshold needed to maintain consistent delivery and optimisation.

When you cut Meta budget below the algorithm's floor, you're not saving money linearly — you're paying a higher cost-per-relevant-lead for worse quality leads. There's a minimum viable budget (approximately ₹2.5–3L/week) below which Meta becomes structurally inefficient for this use case.
The calling gap: pipeline left on the table
This isn't a platform failure but it shows up in every platform's data. App leads: 11.6% connect rate, 51% relevant when reached. Website: 4.3% connect rate, 54% relevant when reached. 16 New Leads (never called) have pulled portfolios above ₹50L worth ₹75.6 Cr combined.

Pooja Kapoor (₹51.6 Cr pulled, New Lead, appeared in week 01/02–07/02) has never been called. She has been in the system for over 60 days.

This is a ₹75 Cr gap that exists entirely because of process failure, not marketing failure. No additional spend required.
